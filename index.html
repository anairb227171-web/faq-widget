Accessible FAQ (Text Version)

General Questions

Q: When and where is the Disability Pride Parade happening?
A: Sunday, October 19th, from Madison Square Park to Union Square Park in Manhattan.

Q: Is the Festival free to attend?
A: Yes! The festival is free and open to everyone.

Q: Do I need to register in advance?
A: To set up a table, or if you need any accommodations, yes. Volunteers and people who are tabling will need to register in advance, but the parade is open to the public.

Q: What is the Disability Parade and Awareness Event all about?
A: Honoring Disability Pride by uniting diverse disabled and non-disabled communities through common experiences, performances, and open conversation.

Q: How do I get to the festival?
A: Many options via public transportation (see MTA). For Access-A-Ride, contact them 1–2 days in advance.

Q: Are there restrooms available?
A: Yes, porta potties along the parade, and public restrooms at Union Park.

Q: Will there be Disability Pride NYC merchandise?
A: TBD.

Q: Will there be food and vendors?
A: Yes, vendors throughout the parade.

Q: Will there be water stations?
A: Yes, across different blocks to keep you hydrated.

Q: Is parking available?
A: No designated parking. Street parking or public transit is recommended.

Q: Where can I sign up to volunteer?
A: Use the 2025 Parade Registration Form.

Accessibility

Q: What accessibility accommodations will be available?
A: ASL interpreters, live captioning, wheelchair accessible routes, sensory guides, etc.

Q: Are service animals allowed?
A: Yes, service animals are always permitted.

A: Email [insert accessibility contact email]. Please make requests at least [insert timeframe] before the event.
